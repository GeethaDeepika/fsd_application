What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, essential for vision.

Common Symptoms

  • Gradual loss of peripheral vision
  • Eye pain and headaches
  • Blurred vision
  • Rainbow-colored halos around lights

Risk Factors

  • Age over 60
  • Certain medical conditions
  • Family history
  • High eye pressure

Did You Know?

  • Over 3 million Americans have glaucoma, but only half know they have it.
  • Glaucoma is the second leading cause of blindness worldwide.
  • Early detection and treatment can prevent vision loss.
  • There is no cure for glaucoma, but medication and surgery can help slow its progression.

Diabetic Retinopathy

Understanding DR

Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) is a diabetes complication affecting the blood vessels in the retina, which can lead to vision loss.

Treatment Options

  • Laser Treatment
  • Anti-VEGF Therapy
  • Blood Sugar Control
  • Regular Eye Check-ups

Stages of DR

  • Mild NPDR: Small swelling in retinal blood vessels.
  • Moderate NPDR: Some blood vessels get blocked.
  • Severe NPDR: Large blockages cause retina damage.
  • Proliferative DR: Fragile new blood vessels form, increasing the risk of bleeding.
  • Advanced DR: Significant vision loss due to bleeding and scarring.

Did You Know?

  • DR is the leading cause of blindness in adults.
  • Early detection can prevent up to 95% of vision loss cases.
  • Proper diabetes management significantly reduces the risk of DR.
  • High blood pressure increases the risk of diabetic retinopathy.
  • Annual eye exams are crucial for diabetic patients.
