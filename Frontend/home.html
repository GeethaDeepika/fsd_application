Understanding Of Glaucoma

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often caused by high pressure in the eye. It can lead to permanent vision loss if untreated.

Common Symptoms

  • Gradual loss of peripheral vision
  • Eye pain and headaches
  • Blurred or cloudy vision
  • Halos around lights

Risk Factors

  • Age over 60
  • Medical conditions (e.g., diabetes, hypertension)
  • Family history of glaucoma
  • High eye pressure

Did You Know?

  • Over 3 million Americans have glaucoma, but only half are aware of it.
  • It’s the second leading cause of blindness globally.
  • Early detection can prevent significant vision loss.
  • While there’s no cure, treatments can slow its progression.

Diabetic Retinopathy

Understanding DR

Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) is a serious complication of diabetes that affects the retina — the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye. It occurs when high blood sugar levels cause damage to blood vessels in the retina, leading to swelling, leakage, or abnormal blood vessel growth.

Stages of DR

  • Mild DR: Small swelling in retinal blood vessels.
  • Moderate DR: Some blood vessels get blocked.
  • Severe DR: Large blockages cause retina damage.
  • Proliferative DR: Fragile new blood vessels increase bleeding risk.
  • Advanced DR: Vision loss due to bleeding and scarring.

Did You Know?

  • DR is the leading cause of blindness in working-age adults.
  • Early detection can prevent up to 95% of vision loss cases.
  • Good diabetes management reduces DR risk significantly.
  • High blood pressure increases DR progression.
