Understanding Glaucoma

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, essential for good vision.

Common Symptoms

  • Gradual loss of peripheral vision
  • Eye pain and headaches
  • Blurred vision
  • Rainbow-colored halos around lights

Risk Factors

  • Age over 60
  • Family history
  • High eye pressure
  • Certain medical conditions

Diabetic Retinopathy

Understanding Diabetic Retinopathy

A diabetes complication affecting blood vessels in the retina, potentially leading to vision loss.

Stages of DR

1
Mild NPDR

Small areas of balloon-like swelling in retina's blood vessels

2
Moderate NPDR

Some blood vessels that nourish the retina become blocked

3
Severe NPDR

More blood vessels become blocked, leading to areas of the retina being deprived of blood supply

Treatment Options

  • Laser Treatment
  • Anti-VEGF Therapy
  • Vitrectomy
  • Regular Monitoring
